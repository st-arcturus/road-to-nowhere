Road to Nowhere

3–5 players  |  45–60 minutes  |  designed by st_arcturus

Components

  • 1 map (11"×17")
  • 6 company charter mats (8.5"×5.5")
  • 50 discs (10 in each of 5 player colors)
  • 42 share cards (7 in each of 6 company colors)
  • 156 cubes (26 in each of 6 company colors)
  • $200 in poker chips or similar (in $1s and $5s)

Setup

  • Place the map on a table. If playing at 3/4 players cover the top 5/3 rows of hexes.
  • Companies equal to players +1 will be in play. Place 1 cube from each company in the active box on the map.
  • Place 1 charter for each company next to the map. Place 25 cubes of the same color on the road track of each charter. Place 7 share cards matching the cube color in the shares section of each charter.
  • Give each player 10 discs in one color and $25/$30/$35 (at 3/4/5 players). Place 1 disc from each player in a random order on the turn track; this is the initial turn order.
  • Each player draws 1 share at random from a pool of 1 share from each company. Then, in reverse turn order, each player chooses a second share (of a different color from their first) from a pool of 1 share from each company.

Overview

  • The game is played in rounds. There are 4 phases in a round. Players act in turn order in every phase.
    1. Bid for Turn Order for the rest of this round and the first phase of the next.
    2. Buy Shares which are worth [roads company built ÷ 2] (round up) at game end.
    3. Build Roads with build points. Companies become inactive if they build in 2 cities or can never do so.
    4. Claim Land which, if built on, is worth [company's treasury ÷ claims company built on] at game end.
  • The game ends if, after the Build Roads phase, there are fewer than 2 companies still active.
  • The player with the most money wins. There are 3 sources of money: shares, land claims, and leftover cash.

Sequence of Play

  1. Bid for Turn Order
    • Raise the bid or pass. When you pass, place one of your discs on the lowest empty space on the turn track.
    • Continue until all but one player has passed. That player places a token on the 1st player spot on the turn track.
  2. Buy Shares
    • If you made a bid in the previous phase, place a cube from the active box onto your spot on the turn track.
    • Pay your bid if you're the 1st player, or half of it (round up) if you're not, into that treasury and take a share.
    • Once all eligible players have taken from the active box, or it is empty, return all cubes on the turn track there.
      • If you made a bid but did not get a share because the active box was emptied, keep your entire bid.
  3. Build Roads
    • Place a cube from the active box on your spot on the turn track.
    • Starting with you, and continuing in turn order, wrapping around the turn track if required, all shareholders must build roads of that company's color until they can no longer do so.
      • Shareholders have 1 build point per share they have in the building company.
      • A company becomes inactive if it ever builds in a second city or is permanently unable to do so.
        • Immediately stop building its roads and remove its cube from the turn track or active box.
    • Once all players have taken from the active box, or it is empty, return all cubes on the turn track there.
      • If there are now fewer than 2 cubes in the active box, the game ends.
  4. Claim Land
    • Place one of your discs on any non-city hex that does not contain a cube or a disc.
    • Once all players have placed a disc the round ends and a new round begins.

Road Building

  • Take cubes from the left side of the company's road track when building roads. The highest revealed number on a road track is the value of that company's shares, i.e. [roads company built ÷ 2].
  • The first road a company ever builds must be in a city. Multiple companies can start in the same city.
    • A company can start in a city that immediately forces it to become inactive.
  • Subsequent roads must be built adjacent to the last built road, but no other, of the building company.
  • The default cost of building a road is 1 build point. The default capacity of a hex is 1 road and 1 disc.
  • Different terrain types have different costs and/or capacities.
    • City: Has a capacity of 1 cube from each company and 0 discs.
    • Mountain: Costs 2 build points per hex.
    • Desert: Costs the company $1 per hex. The money leaves the game. Cannot build without paying.
    • River: A company cannot build in a river if the last road they built was in a river.
  • If a company builds on a hex containing a player's disc, remove the disc and place it in the claims section of that company's charter.

Game End

  • The game ends if, immediately after the Build Roads phase ends, there are fewer than 2 companies still active.
  • The winner is the player with the most money. There are three sources of money.
    • Shares: Worth [roads company built ÷ 2] (round up).
    • Land Claims: Worth, if built on, [company's treasury ÷ claims company built on] (round up).
    • Cash: Worth face value.
  • Tiebreakers: fewest shares owned, then fewest land claims built on, then shared victory.
